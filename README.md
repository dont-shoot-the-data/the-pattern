# The Pattern

**Today, California released its 2025 crime reports. Here's what they say.** Published July 1, 2026.

**Live site: [dont-shoot-the-data.github.io/the-pattern](https://dont-shoot-the-data.github.io/the-pattern/)**

All six California DOJ 2025 annual reports (Crime in California, Hate Crime, Homicide, Juvenile Justice, Use of Force, and SB 54), read together on one page. Every number comes from the state's own PDFs.

## What's inside

- **2025 statewide arrests**: felony suspects by race for homicide, rape, robbery, and kidnapping, set against population, with disproportionality multipliers on every chart
- **25 years of data (2000 to 2025)**: the full statewide arrest trend, with the 2015 launch of Kamala Harris' mandated police implicit-bias training marked on the line. Black homicide arrest share: 4.4x population before the training, 4.4x a decade later
- **Conviction funnels**: 61.2% of adult felony dispositions end in conviction; hate crimes end in a hate-crime conviction in 3.7% of reported events
- **Hate crime**: 1,955 events, 1 murder, 0 rapes in the entire year. 1,912 suspects counted, no race breakdown of them anywhere in the report
- **Use of force**: 127 civilian deaths published by race with no armed/unarmed split, against the national benchmark that 1 in 18 people killed by police is unarmed ([Washington Post Fatal Force](https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/investigations/police-shootings-database/), 2015 to 2024)
- **The Pattern**: six documented instances across the series of precision where the numbers flatter and generality where they don't

## Method

Suspects, never victims. Hispanic is never folded into White. Disproportionality = arrest share divided by population share. A source modal links all 26 annual Crime in California reports (2000 to 2025) for line-by-line fact-checking.

## Sources

California DOJ 2025 report series; annual Crime in California reports 2000 to 2024; Washington Post Fatal Force database 2015 to 2024; U.S. Census ACS population estimates.
